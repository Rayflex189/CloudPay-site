Terms and Terminologies

Here you will find the standard terms and terminologies used by our company.

1. Account Types

Welcome to our Banking Terms and Terminologies section. Here you'll find explanations for common terms used in banking and financial services. Savings Account: A deposit account that earns interest on the balance maintained, usually for personal savings. Checking Account: An account used for daily transactions, allowing withdrawals, deposits, and check-writing. CD (Certificate of Deposit): A time deposit with a fixed term and fixed interest rate, generally higher than regular savings accounts.

2. Fees and Charges

ATM Fee: A charge for using an ATM that belongs to another bank or network. Overdraft Fee: Fee charged when the account balance goes below zero due to a transaction. Maintenance Fee: A monthly charge for maintaining a bank account, sometimes waived if certain conditions are met.

3. Transactions

Deposit: Adding funds to an account, usually through cash, check, or electronic transfer. Withdrawal: Removing funds from an account, typically done via ATM, branch, or electronic transfer. Transfer: Moving funds from one account to another, within the same bank or to another bank.

4. Loan and Credit

Mortgage: A loan to purchase real estate, secured by the property itself. Credit Score: A numerical representation of an individual's creditworthiness, used by lenders to assess risk. Interest Rate: The cost of borrowing money, expressed as a percentage.

5. Security and Fraud

PIN (Personal Identification Number): A numeric password used to authenticate ATM and debit card transactions. Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information (like passwords or credit card numbers) by masquerading as a trustworthy entity. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): A security process requiring two forms of identification before granting access to an account.
